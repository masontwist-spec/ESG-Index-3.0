FTSE 100 ESG Exposure Index

A contrarian framework for ranking FTSE 100 companies by their ESG exposure. Higher values reflect greater commitments, incentives, programmes, disclosure intensity, and related governance integration. Lower scores therefore rank higher on the Index.

How to Read the Site

Each page applies the same contrarian interpretation: lower scores rank higher. Use the Environment and Social pages to inspect the live domain-specific drivers, and the ESG page for the composite public-facing score.

Environment

The Environment page shows Climate Targets, Investment & Transition, and Climate Reporting.

Social

The Social page shows DEI Targets & Representation, DEI Programmes & Memberships, and Social Incentives.

ESG

The ESG page uses an Environment + Social public composite, while governance-related indicators are treated as embedded rather than scored as a standalone public-facing domain.

Construction note: This is not a traditional ESG “quality” index. It is an ESG exposure / burden index. Under the current public model, more commitments, more programmes, more incentives, and more disclosure intensity increase the score.
