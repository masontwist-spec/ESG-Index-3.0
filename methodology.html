Methodology

The FTSE 100 ESG Exposure Index is a contrarian framework. Higher values reflect greater ESG-related commitments, programmes, incentives, and reporting intensity. Lower scores therefore rank higher on the Index.

Structure: The public-facing website is built around two live domains — Environment and Social. Each domain contains three sub-domains. Governance-related indicators are treated as embedded within these live frameworks rather than scored as a standalone public-facing domain.

Environment

Three sub-domains stemming from the Environment domain.

E1 — Climate Targets

  • Net zero commitment year: earlier years receive higher inverted scores.
  • Scope coverage: 1+2+3 scores above narrower coverage.
  • SBTi validation: stronger pathways score higher.
  • TPI alignment is normalized by dividing level by the framework maximum.

E2 — Investment & Transition

  • Decarbonisation capex increases the score as plans and spending intensify.
  • Green bonds increase the score with larger issuance bands.
  • RE100 / EP100 / EV100 participation raises the score.
  • Internal carbon price disclosure raises the score.

E3 — Climate Reporting

  • Sustainability report extent contributes to the reporting score.
  • CDP grades map into numeric values.
  • Climate KPIs in executive pay raise the score.
  • Third-party assurance increases the score, with reasonable above limited.

Social

Three sub-domains stemming from the Social domain.

S1 — DEI Targets & Representation

  • Gender and ethnicity targets contribute to the score as they become more formal and explicit.
  • Board and leadership representation increase the score at higher thresholds.
  • Workforce representation contributes where disclosure shows stronger representation outcomes.
  • Target deadline strength raises the score where commitments are earlier and more concrete.

S2 — Programmes & Memberships

  • DEI memberships raise the score as companies participate in more formal external initiatives.
  • Programmes such as executive-level DEI roles, ERGs, and formal training increase the score.
  • External participation and internal programme intensity are treated as additive signals.
  • Higher programme density contributes to a higher overall social exposure score.

S3 — Social Governance & Incentives

  • Diversity linked to executive pay raises the score, with stronger linkage scoring higher.
  • Board oversight contributes where DEI oversight is formal and clearly structured.
  • Senior management target commitments raise the score where explicit commitments are present.
  • Incentive and oversight structures together increase the social governance exposure score.

Governance

Governance is acknowledged explicitly, but treated as embedded rather than scored as a standalone public-facing domain.

Governance Treatment

Governance-related indicators are treated as embedded within the live Environment and Social frameworks rather than presented as a standalone public-facing score. In the underlying methodology, governance appears through strategy, oversight, incentives, transparency, and assurance-related signals. These include ESG integration into corporate strategy and risk management, board-level oversight structures, diversity-linked incentives, formal committees, external frameworks, and third-party assurance.

For the public-facing website, those governance signals are reflected primarily through the reporting, oversight, and incentive-related measures already captured inside the Environment and Social domains. This keeps governance visible in the logic of the index without displaying it as a separate scored pillar.

How to Read the Scores

This is not a traditional ESG “quality” index. Under the current framework, more commitments, more programmes, more incentives, and more extensive reporting increase the score. Lower values therefore rank higher.

Public-Facing ESG Composite

The current public-facing ESG score shown on the website is built from the live Environment and Social modules. It is intended as a readable composite rather than a full standalone three-pillar public model.

Governance Treatment

Governance-related indicators are embedded within the Environment and Social frameworks, especially in reporting, oversight, and incentive-related measures, rather than displayed as a standalone public-facing scoring domain.
