Methodology

The FTSE 100 ESG Exposure Index is a contrarian framework. Higher values reflect greater commitments, programmes, incentives, reporting intensity, or related governance integration. Lower scores therefore rank higher on the Index.

Environment

  • Climate Targets captures target setting, scope coverage, and related climate commitments.
  • Investment & Transition captures transition actions, spending, and related climate-oriented commitments.
  • Climate Reporting captures disclosures, reporting intensity, KPIs, and assurance-related indicators.

Social

  • DEI Targets & Representation captures formal targets and representation-related exposure.
  • DEI Programmes & Memberships captures memberships, programmes, and related participation indicators.
  • Social Incentives captures incentive structures, oversight, and related social-governance features.

Composite ESG

  • The ESG page uses the current composite total score from the full scoring structure.
  • The Companies page shows Environment, Social, and composite ESG side by side.
  • All rankings are displayed in ascending order, so lower scores rank higher.
Construction note: This is not a traditional ESG “quality” index. It is an ESG exposure / burden index. More commitments, more programmes, more reporting, and more integrated incentives or oversight increase the score. Lower values are preferred under the current framework.

How to Use the Pages

The site is structured so that users can move from domain-specific views into the combined ranking.

Environment Page

Use the Environment page to inspect the climate-related sub-pillars and the total Environment score.

Social Page

Use the Social page to inspect the Social sub-pillars and the total Social score.

ESG Page

Use the ESG page for the overall composite ranking and the Companies page for a simpler directory view across all three score types.
