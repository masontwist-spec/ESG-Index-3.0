Methodology

The FTSE 100 ESG Exposure Index is a contrarian framework. Higher values reflect greater ESG-related commitments, spending, policies, and reporting intensity. Lower scores therefore rank higher on the Index.

Current Live Module

  • The Environment module is the current live scoring framework on the website.
  • Rankings are shown in ascending order, so lower Environment scores rank better.
  • The current Companies page also reflects Environment scores only.

Environment Structure

  • E1 — Climate Targets: target setting, scope coverage, and related climate commitments.
  • E2 — Investment & Transition: transition spending, capital allocation, and related initiatives.
  • E3 — Climate Reporting: disclosures, reporting intensity, KPIs, and assurance-related indicators.

Roadmap

  • The Social module is the next major addition to the site.
  • After Social is launched, a full composite ESG page will be added.
  • Governance-related elements may be embedded selectively rather than treated as a standalone major page.
Construction note: This is not a traditional ESG “quality” index. It is an ESG exposure / burden index. Under the current framework, more commitments, more policy intensity, and more reporting increase the score. Lower values are preferred and rank higher.
